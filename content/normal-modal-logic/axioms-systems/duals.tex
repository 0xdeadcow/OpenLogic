\olsection{Dual Schemas}

\begin{defn}\ollabel{def:duals}
  Each of the schemas \Ax{T}, \Ax{B}, \Ax{4}, and
  \Ax{5} has a \emph{dual}, denoted by a subscripted diamond, as
  follows:

  \begin{tabular}{p{1cm}l}%@{\hspace{1cm}}l}
    $\Ax{T_\Diamond}$ : & $!A \lif \Diamond!A$\\
    $\Ax{B_\Diamond}$ : & $\Diamond\Box!A \lif !A$\\
    $\Ax{4_\Diamond}$ : & $\Diamond\Diamond!A \lif \Diamond!A$\\
    $\Ax{5_\Diamond}$ : & $\Diamond\Box!A \lif \Box!A$
    \end{tabular}
\end{defn}

Each of the dual above schemas is obtained from the corresponding
schema by replacing $\lnot !A$ for $!A$, contraposing, and
re-writing. Schema \Ax{D} is its own dual (modulo the replacement
of $\lnot \Diamond \lnot$ by $\Box$). 

\begin{prob}
  Show that for each schema $!A$ in \olref[mod][prf][dua]{def:duals}:
  $\Log{K} \Proves !A \liff !A_\Diamond$.
\end{prob}
